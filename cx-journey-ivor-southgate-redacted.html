What Led to the Non-Reply Email

The customer's journey was derailed by a specific sequence: automated emails were sent from no-reply@booking.com and cs-noreply@booking.com while he was actively trying to resolve a refund. Here's what caused it:

  1. Property went silent - Ibis asked for a flight itinerary on 30 April, I. replied same day, then nothing. No SLA enforcement on property response times.
  2. Case entered automated queue - With no property resolution, the case sat in the system until 7 May when auto-sends triggered without human review.
  3. Auto-sends fired from no-reply addresses - The "information request" and "about your recent enquiry" templates all came from mailboxes the customer cannot reply to.
  4. Customer replied anyway - I. responded to the auto-sends with his frustration, but those replies either bounced or went into a void with no routing back to a human.
  5. No feedback loop exists - When a customer tries to reply to a no-reply email, there is no mechanism to detect this, flag it, or route it to an agent. The conversation simply dies.

The friction points: The system sent emails that looked like they came from a person (signed "V.", "K.") but from addresses no one can reply to. This creates a false expectation of dialogue. When the customer's only response path is blocked, they escalate - first to named contacts, then to executives.

Critical Failures Summary
Critical
🔁
Duplicate Auto-Sends With No Deduplication
The system fired identical email templates multiple times within the same session (75 minutes). There is no deduplication logic preventing the same template from being sent twice to the same customer for the same case. This is the most visible symptom of the broken auto-send infrastructure.
Critical
🗃️
Auto-Sends Request Data the System Already Holds
The "information request" template asks for confirmation number, email, accommodation name, and check-in/out dates - all of which exist in booking #654XXX0103. Templates are not validated against booking data before sending. The system appears to have no memory of who the customer is.
Critical
💬
No Conversation-State Suppression
Auto-responses fire regardless of whether an active dialogue is in progress. Agent K. asks for information on 25 May while I. is mid-conversation about the same issue. The system has no concept of "this customer is currently engaged" that would suppress generic auto-sends.
High
📊
Lifecycle Emails Ignore Booking State
Satisfaction surveys ("how was your stay?") were sent for a booking that was cancelled and never used. The cancellation state does not propagate to suppress downstream lifecycle communications. The system treats a cancelled, disputed booking the same as a completed stay.
High
🚫
No Escalation Detection or Sentiment Routing
After I. explicitly stated "stop wasting my time" on 7 May, subsequent interactions on 25 May still triggered the same generic auto-response pattern. There is no feedback loop from customer tone/sentiment to communication strategy. Escalation signals are ignored.
High
🧠
Zero Case Continuity Across Interactions
Agent K. (25 May) has no visibility of what happened on 7 May or any prior interaction. Each contact starts from zero - same questions, same templates, no progress. There is no shared case thread that accumulates context over time.
Medium
📭
No-Reply Addresses Block Customer Response
Auto-sends come from no-reply@booking.com and cs-noreply@booking.com. When the customer wants to respond to a specific message or correct an error, the channel is closed. This forces re-escalation through a different path with no context carried forward.
Medium
📞
Phone Deflection During Active Email Dialogue
One of the 5 auto-sends on 7 May offered phone numbers as the resolution path - while the customer was actively engaged in an email dialogue about a refund. Channel switching as a deflection tactic mid-conversation adds friction without resolution.
Connection to UCC Program

James asked whether Pega can help us fix automated sends sooner. This links directly to the Written Channels migration. The numbers show this isn't an outlier:

  • 90% of our CS email templates are no-reply - the dead-end that trapped this customer
  • Email CSAT sits at 49.2% - the lowest-scoring channel, and the one where this played out
  • 81.8% email re-contact rate - this customer contacted us 8+ times because auto-sends created more questions than answers
  • 54% of agent contacts are status lookups - this customer's need (refund a cancelled booking) could have been resolved without an agent

If we migrate this logic to AWS Connect as-is, we move the problem to new infrastructure without fixing it. Designing Connect with conversation awareness from day one - knowing when a customer is mid-dialogue, what data we already hold, and when to stay silent - would stop journeys like this before they start.
