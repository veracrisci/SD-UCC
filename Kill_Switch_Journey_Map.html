Emergency Service Controls

Scenario: RTA activates Kill Switch and support channel is disabled
During critical operational events (system outages, unexpected volume spikes, or staffing emergencies) RTA immediately disables a specific language-channel combination. A traveler reaches out for support but the channel is no longer available. Rather than hitting a dead-end, the system intercepts the interaction before queue placement and provides a clear, guided redirection to an alternative channel.

This journey maps what happens to the traveler before, during, and after a channel is disabled, specifically showing two parallel paths depending on whether the kill switch is OFF (normal operations) or ON (customer redirected).

Important note: the flow logic is channel-agnostic as it applies regardless of whether the traveler enters via Live Chat or Voice.

Design Constraints

Kill Switch Activation Mid-Interaction

If the kill switch is turned ON while the customer is already on a chat/call, the ongoing interaction will continue uninterrupted. The kill switch impact is always and only before the customer is placed in the agent queue. Active sessions are never terminated by kill switch activation.

Open Questions

Question 1 - Identification stage

Welcome message to be adjusted when kill switch is ON: remove "Please send a brief message to let us know how we can help, so that we can connect you with the right team member as quickly as possible." from original message.

Question 2 - Classification stage

Copy for the prompt/system message when kill switch is ON to be defined by copywriter. Current copy is placeholder.

Question 3 - Classification stage (Voice)

All prompts when moving the customer to another language queue must be played in the preferred language of the customer, not the default queue language.

Question 4 - General

Define with RTA what are the scenarios when this kill switch will be used, to ensure this CX is applicable and to feed insights for copy generation.
