Translation Experience Journey

Scenario: Traveller contacts support - no native-language agent available
A traveller reaches out for support in their preferred language - but no agent who speaks that language is available. Rather than forcing the traveller into English or routing them to a longer queue, the system activates translation to bridge the gap. This journey maps what happens from that moment forward: the primary service interaction, but also what follows (outbound calls to partner, escalations or consultation, follow-ups and re-contact) - where language continuity needs to hold across channels and touchpoints.
Owner: Vera Crisci - Service Design
Each stage includes the relevant Experience Principles and requirements mapped to the North Star Blueprint.

Traveller
  • Communicates in preferred language throughout the session
  • Sees agent replies translated into their language
  • Messages appear in the language they typed - no automatic alteration of their own input
  • Quick replies and confirmations delivered in their language
  • AI-translated label visible (non-intrusive)
  • Translation occurs in real-time - latency must not disrupt the flow of conversation
  • System handles typos, abbreviations, and informal language without breaking translation
  • Proper nouns (traveller names, hotel names, locations) are never translated
  • Regional language differences respected (e.g. Mexican Spanish vs EU Spanish, Brazilian Portuguese vs EU Portuguese)
  • Can indicate they do not understand a translated message - agent is notified and can rephrase or simplify
  • System monitors translation confidence scores per message - if score drops below acceptable level, traveller is offered a fallback (native-language agent or alternative channel)
  • Shared landing pages (e.g. policy pages, confirmation screens) displayed in their preferred language
Agent

  • Sees traveller's message translated into English - can click "show original" to view the untranslated message

  • Can flag wrong or misleading translations for quality review
  • Can preview how message will appear in traveller's language
  • Makes outbound calls to partners/hotels during session to resolve
  • AI co-pilot suggests responses adapted to the traveller's language and cultural context (e.g. tone, formality, phrasing)

  • Translation quality score linked to interaction record
  • Flagged translations feed back into model improvement
